Interface Redesign - Steam Application

A Student Project

Tools + Skills practiced

  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • User-center Design
  • Heuristic Evaluation
  • User Interviews and Research

What I learned

The company we looked into is a small, local Vancouver company that creates 100% Canadian Maple Decks for skateboarders. They currently sell their boards in bulk to other local skateboard brands such as Sitka while providing a low maintenance website for odd jobs here and there.

The Details

What is this about?

In this project, I worked with user-center design methods along with proper heuristic evaluation of a current platform. Using Adobe Illustrator and proper testing methods [ Interviews, usability testing] we were able to come up with a design that was better than the original [according to results, mind you, we used only 6 participants for each version we produced].

Below are some scanned images of my quick sketches of potential layouts of how the re-designed Steam could look. This project, the group really wanted to ease and streamline the ability to make comments, and to get notified when someone else commented.

Why do this?

Because we wanted to focus on streamlining the ability to comment and keep track of commented pages, I drew the comments tab and group activities tab along with friends[and your own] game recommendations. The right side, used to be a list of links. I incorporated larger icons to ensure users gave attention to this side of the screen. Everything below is similar to the original steam profile page [Before its revamp].

Wait a minute, they don't JUST wait. They also get to access a new section that we felt was lacking in any Skateboarding website. A section where local skateboarders or Icons can gather and reinforce their already cohesive community.
